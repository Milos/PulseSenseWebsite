Support

Last updated: May 28, 2026

Need help with Pulse Sense? Email menix.dev@gmail.com and include your iPhone model, iOS version, strap firmware version if visible, and a short description of what happened.

Compatibility

Pulse Sense is built for compatible Polar H10 chest straps and iPhone. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Polar Electro Oy.

Common setup steps

  • Wear the H10 strap with moistened electrodes before scanning.
  • Keep the strap close to your iPhone during first connection.
  • Allow Bluetooth permission when iOS asks.
  • Allow Apple Health permission if you want workouts and heart-rate samples saved to Apple Health.

If the strap is not found

  • Check that the strap is being worn and has battery power.
  • Move the strap close to the iPhone.
  • Disconnect the strap from other active apps if it is already connected elsewhere.
  • Restart Bluetooth or restart the iPhone if discovery does not recover.

If Apple Health sync does not complete

  • Open iOS Settings and confirm Pulse Sense has permission to write workouts, heart rate, and active energy to Health.
  • Open the Health app and check whether the workout already appears there.
  • Return to Pulse Sense and retry sync from the workout summary or workout history.

If workouts do not restore from iCloud

  • Confirm the iPhone is signed in to the same Apple ID used when the workouts were backed up.
  • Confirm iCloud is enabled and the device has an internet connection.
  • Open Pulse Sense and leave it open briefly so the app can check for private iCloud backups.
  • If workouts still do not appear, contact support and mention that iCloud restore did not complete.
